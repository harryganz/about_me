Why I Program

I learned the statistical programming language, R, as part of a course on biostatistics. It was immensely useful, and I taught myself as much as I could about R. It is a very high-level langauge and cannot be used to do many useful things on a computer. To expand my knowledge of computer science I took a course on Java, and probably would have taken more CS courses, but they conflicted with courses I needed for my major. In the meantime, I taught myself Python, which seemed to be a useful langauge, but I wasn't doing much coding on a regular basis.
When I started working for NOAA, I needed to teach myself SQL and some web framework to send data over the internet. I decided on Ruby on Rails, and wrote an R package as a front-end to analyze the data client-side. For the past year I have been managing the Reef Visual Census data, and developing tools to disseminate and analyze it over the internet. With my contract expiring on a few weeks, I have had a difficult time finding a job in marine science, and am now considering entering web develpment full-time.
