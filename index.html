About Me

I am new to the whole web development scene, and entered it through a bit of a back door. As an undergraduate at Cornell University I studied biology, and was mostly interested in population dynamics. As part of my curriculum I learned some programming to help with modeling and simulation. I was very interested in programming, but continued to focus on biology. It wasn't until I started using Ruby on Rails as part of my work for NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Association) that I started seriously considering switching to developing software full-time. While working at NOAA, I taught myself the Ruby on Rails framework, and, for good measure, Node.js, AngularJS, and Express frameworks as well.

As of writing this, I have only made a few apps with each framework, mostly to teach myself the different technologies, but I think that I am ready to take the next step and make "real" web applications. I would like to be something like a junior developer on a team, learning and improving as I go. I think I would be a valuable asset to any team who need someone who can learn and adapt quickly, and without entrenched bad habits.
